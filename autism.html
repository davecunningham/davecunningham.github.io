Key challenges
Formatting output into the required ADIR structure required external support — contributed to a 2/5 ease rating for that specific entry.
Initial concerns about quiet voices not being captured — resolved successfully in practice for most sessions.
Child kept trying to touch the phone, stopping the recording — physical placement needs consideration.
Some clinicians still exploring which templates work best — ease ratings reflect varying familiarity with the system.

Accuracy / Hallucination Concerns

  • Mixed up the patient's brother and father as speakers in the transcript.
  • When participants spoke in Polish, Magic Notes generated fabricated English content — attributed a statement about "brain games" to the patient which she had not said. Careful post-session checking was required.
  • One entry noted incorrect details added via the "change bar" feature — attributed to the learning curve.
  • No accuracy issues reported across the remaining 8 entries. System captured speech clearly even for a young person with speech clarity difficulties.

What worked well — collating questionnaires & verbatim quotes
Being able to add the parent information, school information etc into the panel form was a MASSIVE time save.
Much quicker than previously where I would have to collate and type all the info from teacher and parents.
Verbatim entries saved me dictating quotes — could cut and paste instead.
Collates all the background information into the correct headings much more quickly.
Key challenges
When merging multiple sources, information blurs across headings and requires extensive cross-referencing — often more time-consuming than dictating directly.
Template merges clinical observations with school and parent info, requiring manual separation. A blank template option was frequently requested.
System writes up "not a problem" items, lengthening panel reports and increasing reading time.
Clinician abbreviations used in output — not acceptable in medical reports. Output also lacks dates, considered unprofessional.

Accuracy / Hallucination Concerns

  • System assumed diagnostic criteria were met from limited input — stated "significant impairments in functioning" when no parent, teacher, or observation data had yet been added.
  • Stated that a school observation had taken place when it had not.
  • Single-word errors changed meaning entirely (e.g. "did" vs "didn't") — requires careful checking throughout.
  • Strong referral symptoms were removed or diluted in summarisation, risking impact on panel diagnostic decisions.
  • Inaccurate number of session attendees recorded (6 people counted as 3).
  • One entry produced entirely inaccurate output — no usable content generated from the session.
