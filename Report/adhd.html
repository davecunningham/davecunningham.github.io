Supporting comments
This could be a game changer in terms of our assessments. Report writing and collating information is incredibly time consuming.
After one session I had completed a significant portion of the assessment report — it was written up before I stepped out of the room.
I felt very happy when I saw how much info it had captured in an assessment with someone with ADHD who spoke fast and went off on tangents.
More comprehensive information available to import into report — reduced cognitive load. Easy to pull out key info from child health notes that may have been missed.

Key challenges
Tone can be overly cold and blunt about events described by the client — needed editing to make the report more empathetic and person-centred.
Used "they/their" throughout instead of the correct pronoun — easy to change but requires a careful read-through.
Repetitive sentences appeared across entries — noted by two separate clinicians.
Nearly forgot to pause recording when leaving the room — a reminder or auto-pause feature would reduce risk of recording continuing unattended.
Did not handle document dates correctly — reported historical documents as if they were current, which is misleading.
