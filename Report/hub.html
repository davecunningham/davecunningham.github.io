Context

About this programme

Two health boards, different methods, shared purpose.

Why AI scribes in ND services?

Clinicians in neurodevelopmental services spend a disproportionate amount of time on documentation — often reconstructing sessions from memory after back-to-back appointments. This fragments clinical attention, increases error risk, and contributes to burnout. AI scribes like Magic Notes capture sessions in real time, producing structured notes that clinicians review and sign off — giving time back for direct care.

How the evidence was collected

  • CTM pilot: 5 clinicians, 6 weeks, 726 tasks — structured before/after time comparisons, ease ratings, and detailed qualitative feedback across every task type.
  • Hywel Dda diary study: Clinicians in Autism and ADHD teams completed diary entries per session — recording time, ease, quality judgement, and a dedicated hallucination question.
  • Both methods were designed to be lightweight enough not to add burden, while capturing enough data to make a governance case.

Policy context

This programme sits within Welsh Government's WHC 2025/026 — the framework for safe and responsible adoption of ambient voice technologies and AI scribes in clinical settings. Each health board does not need to develop its own information governance framework from scratch — this guidance provides the basis for adoption.

What the combined report will cover

  • Quantitative comparison across CTM and Hywel Dda (time saved %, ease, quality)
  • Hallucination rates by task type and LHB — including clinical risk assessment
  • Which task types consistently benefit most from AI scribing
  • Which task types require caution and human verification protocols
  • Recommendations for Wales-wide rollout and governance
⚠ A note on hallucinations across both sites: All three reports document instances where Magic Notes generated inaccurate content. The most clinically significant examples involve medication names (ADHD team regular reviews) and diagnostic assumptions (Autism team panel presentations). Readers of these reports — particularly those making adoption decisions — should pay close attention to the hallucination sections in each team report. The combined report will include a cross-LHB hallucination analysis and recommended checking protocols.
