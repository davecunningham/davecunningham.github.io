About this work

Why this matters

Neurodevelopmental (ND) services in Wales are under significant strain. Demand is rising, waiting lists are long, and the clinicians doing this work carry enormous administrative burden — often at the cost of time with families.

This site preserves the findings from a service design project exploring the entire ND pathway: from first referral through to post-diagnostic follow-up. It documents what families experience, what clinicians need, where the system breaks down, and where innovation is beginning to make a real difference.

The research spans qualitative interviews, pathway mapping, simulation testing, and a real-world pilot of AI scribe technology across the Cwm Taf Morgannwg (CTM) Children's ND Service.

What's not working

Key pain points across the pathway

These pain points were identified through clinician interviews, admin task mapping, and pathway observation across Welsh NHS services.

Referral

Referral forms are long, PDF-based and inconsistent — GPs and schools lack time to gather quality evidence, leading to repeated requests, delays, and unsuitable referrals re-entering the system.

Referral

Bulk school referrals contain duplicated or inconsistent information. Schools receive no feedback on why referrals are accepted or declined, so problems repeat without improvement.

Screening & Triage

Clinicians spend 1–2 hours per case reading long questionnaires from multiple sources. Information is scattered across WelshPAS, CarePartner, spreadsheets and email — requiring manual detective work.

Screening & Triage

Safeguarding and risk information is buried in long documents. There is no early visibility of risk before appointments — meaning sessions can begin without clinicians being adequately prepared.

Assessment

One-hour assessment sessions must cover introduction, risk, observation, questions and documentation simultaneously. Recording across multiple systems during sessions competes directly with clinical observation.

Assessment

Back-to-back sessions with no protected documentation time mean clinicians reconstruct notes from memory, sometimes mixing up details. This creates safeguarding risk and fragile continuity of care.

Admin (Hywel Dda)

Admin staff navigate four disconnected systems — WelshPAS, Care Partner, Excel spreadsheets, and OneDrive — to process a single referral. Total time: 21–69 minutes with significant manual duplication.

Panel

Panel members are expected to have read long reports in advance, but rarely do. Information is re-explained in meetings rather than decisions being made — consuming large volumes of clinician time.

Panel

Panels are used as a substitute for clinical supervision. Historical inconsistency in decisions and high professional consequence weight drives defensive over-documentation and repeated panel attendance.

Reporting

Clinicians spend more time writing reports than seeing children. Manual rewriting of the same content for different audiences (family, GP, school, CAMHS) adds significant workload after every assessment.

Reporting

Post-diagnostic support is inequitable. Diagnosis is prioritised before support, families struggle to process outcomes in a single delivery, and there is no consistent offer of follow-up or signposting.

Caseload visibility

Clinicians manage caseloads through personal spreadsheets and local workarounds. As one clinician put it: "Nobody can see my caseload. If something happens to me, you have nothing."

Pilot recommendations

01
Scale adoption across Welsh ND services
Evidence supports expansion beyond CTM. A standard IG position and governance framework would enable consistent rollout across health boards.
02
Co-design templates with clinicians and families
Templates for school obs, ADOS, supervision and non-diagnosis letters are already requested. Families should be involved in shaping neuro-affirming report language.
03
Standardise Welsh-language support
Welsh-only sessions require dedicated transcription support. This is both a clinical and language-rights requirement for services in Wales.
04
Redesign the service model — not just the tools
AI scribe creates capacity. That capacity should be reinvested in family time, differentiated pathways and proportionate panel use — not absorbed back into backlogs.
05
Extend retention periods to 30–60 days
The pilot's 7-day retention period was set for procurement speed and is too short for clinical workflows. Standard practice in comparable services is 30–60 days.
06
Monitor and report on hallucination rates
Systematic hallucination tracking is essential for governance and for making the case to sceptical health boards. A simple tally per clinician per period would provide sufficient data.
